The story of my Webpage

Having reached the ripe age of 45, I've finally decided what I want to be when I grow up. I've been fortunate to enjoy a successful career in retail management for the last 25 years, and am now looking forward to a second career that focuses more on service to my community. My goal is to complete a Bachelors, and hopefully Master's as well, in Mathematics. Once I complete my degree, I will then pursue teacher's certification, with the goal of teaching at the secondary level in the St Louis city school district. I am completing an Associate's degree in general transfer studies at St Louis Community College this semester, and am very excited by the possibility of completing my bachelors at Washington University.

I began my college education in 1990, majoring in English. Financial issues within my family forced me to pause my studies after one year, and that pause quickly morphed into a full stop. In 2010, I decided to finally restart my studies, and I've taken one or two classes a semester since then. I've gone through a few changes of major changes along the way, but have consistently been drawn back towards a passion for the STEM subjects. Working full-time while pursuing my degree has been a rewarding challenge, and I've greatly enjoyed the self-discipline and time management skills I've developed along the way. I am proud of my grade average, and am graduating as a member of the Dean's list. I have also been a proud member of Phi Theta Kappa.

Math and science explain the relationships within the universe, and I'm consistently dazzled by the numerical dances that make up higher mathematics. Mathematical thinking is a highly beneficial skill that can be applied to almost any endeavor or problem. Whether writing music, coding software, or understanding the global economy, the ability to think mathematically is critical. I would be greatly honored to have Washington University be part of my journey as I work to develop this passion within myself and others.

Washington Univeristy Wikipedia Entry

Washington University in St. Louis (WashU, or WUSTL) is a private research university located in St. Louis, Missouri, United States. Founded in 1853, and named after George Washington, the university has students and faculty from all 50 U.S. states and more than 120 countries.[6] Twenty-five Nobel laureates have been affiliated with Washington University, nine having done the major part of their pioneering research at the university.[7] Washington University's undergraduate program is ranked 19th by U.S. News and World Report[8] and 11th by the Wall Street Journal in 2016.[9] The university is ranked 23rd in the world in 2016 by the Academic Ranking of World Universities.[
